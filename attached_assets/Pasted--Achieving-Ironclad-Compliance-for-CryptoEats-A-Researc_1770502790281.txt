#### 1. Florida Liquor License Verification (Alcohol Delivery Compliance)
From research, Florida allows third-party platforms like CryptoEats to facilitate alcohol delivery under strict rules. Key statutes: FS 561.57 (deliveries by licensed vendors or contractors), FS 565.045 (sealed containers with food orders), and SB 676 (third-party platform rules: consent, transparency, agreements).

**Requirements:**
- **Platform Role:** CryptoEats is a "marketplace provider" (not a direct seller)â€”must register with FL Dept of Revenue (free, Form DR-1).
- **Restaurant Partners:** Only list licensed vendors (verify via FL DBPR license search). Require written consent; disclose fees; provide contact methods and review responses.
- **Deliveries:** Sealed containers; accompanied by food (40%+ of order non-alcohol); 8 AMâ€“10 PM windows; ID verification at delivery (21+ with scan/record); no breweries/distilleries as partners (they can't deliver via third parties).
- **Drivers:** Background checks; training on age verification; independent contractors (see below).
- **Verification Process:** In-app: Auto-check restaurant licenses via API (e.g., integrate FL DBPR database if available); driver scans ID at delivery, app logs photo/timestamp.

**Implementation in App:**
- Onboarding: Restaurants upload license; auto-verify number against state DB.
- Checkout: Flag alcohol orders; require customer DOB; driver app mandates scan before handover.
- Logs: Store verifications in compliance table for audits (7 years retention).

This ensures no fines (up to $500/day for unlicensed ops).
